## Freedom

I must have free time in my life with which I can do whatever I want, without being restricted in a school or being "recommended" to do the "good" stuff, or being assigned a task that I need to finish. This is my right as a human being. I can play computer games, I can watch videos, I can exercise, or I can just sleep. 

I need 3 hours of freedom a day.

## Independence

No one knows what is best for me specifically, no matter who this person is, no matter if he/she is a professor, my parents, the God, myself, or this manual. However, I am the only person responsible for myself. I will not follow everything that is right or "recommended." I will, however, based on my own choice, follow some of the recommended things, as they are indeed helpful for me. These recommended things include but not are not only restricted to academics, schedule, preferences, hobbies. Some people are liers, especially grown-ups like teachers. 

I have the responsibility to decide and choose my own life. I am responsible for my well-being and performances. When I fail, I do not blame it on other people.

## Environment

Environment is largely determined by time and place, and I cannot choose to live 500 years later, nor will I ever go back to 20th century. There are different environment in the world. I try to find the best for me. I try to fit in the environment, and try to make the best out of it. But if there is something I cannot help, then I prioritize my well-being, and leave the environment.

I try to fit in the environment and keeping my independence at the same time.

## Sleep

Long sleep is necessary for my well-being. There is no arguing against it. I prioritize sleeping to anything else in life that does not have an immediate affect on my well-being. Studying until late night doesn't indicate you are studying hard. Sleeping few hours doesn't indicate you are working hard. Getting up early doesn't indicate you haven't got enough sleep. 

I need 9 hours of sleep a day. It is a good idea to sleep from 8 pm to 5 am.

## Hobbies

Hobbies aren't necessarily beneficial--most are a waste of time. I am interested in running, and take that as my hobby. I don't have other regular hobbies because I don't find much joy in doing other things and I don't have much energy left. I run because it is good exercise and I feel happy after running. Running is good for me as long as I don't overexercise. For me running 5k on a daily average is fine. If I am too tired then I can choose to stay at home.

On weekly average, I run 0-5k a day.

## Gaming

Gaming is not beneficial to me in every possible way. Video games are not only harmful for my brain, but also a huge waste of time. I don't usually study after gaming. I usually stay up late after gaming. I usually lose track of time after gaming. THe only reason I game is because it helps me rest. Thus I should stop gaming immediately with every measure possible. Gaming isn't wrong because the video say it is wrong. Gaming isn't wrong because teachers and parents say it is wrong. The videos may be completely wrong for me or completely biased. Teachers and parents may be completely wrong and biased. I think it is wrong myself, and thus I should stop this ineffective thing from ruining my life.

I should stop gaming, however hard it is.

## Friends

I am happy to have friends. If I am doing something together with a friend, I would try to fit him/her preferences and adjust a little more. However, I do not go out of my way only to know other people. 

It is best to have a friend, else, just go on with your life normally.

## Weight

Being medium and thin each have pros and cons, and there is no optimal solution, since weighing the factors differently yields different results.

I hate being fat or really skinny, but I do not have preferences between being thin and medium-weight person.

## Tests

Tests are designed to evaluate performances. Tests do not 100% reflect my ability--I may suck and get 100, or I may get 0 on an easy test. Since I do not design the test, I cannot choose the result. I might have difficulty understanding the problem. I may make lots of mistakes that cannot be avoided. I prepare for every tests carefully and wholeheartedly, trying to score the best I can. But what grades I receive are partly independent with my understandings of the course. In that case that is not my fault, and I should not be affected or blamed.

I try my best on tests, however, I don't care much about results.

## Studying

Studying is a very complicated thing. Studying didn't lead to success, studying didn't lead to understanding the course really well, although they are related to some extent. I might spend little time studying for a hard course and get very good understandings. I might spend much time studying an easy course and get very bad understandings. By understandings I mean self-evalutaion, thus excluding the outer measure of grades. If I cannot understand an easy material after a reasonable time of studying, there is no point in studying any longer. I should come back to it after perhaps, a year. As a student my primary job is studying, and this is the unique thing that makes me stand out. I am different from athlets, musicians, actors, travelors, workers in that I spend much longer time and much more energy studying.
